Learning Objectives

At the conclusion of this knowledge-based educational activity, participants should be able to

  • Distinguish among the currently U.S. approved botulinum toxin products.
  • Identify the major components and rationale of the REMS program for the botulinum toxins.
  • Describe current safety data including information regarding the potential for distant spread of toxin.
  • Summarize and apply recently published evidence regarding the use of botulinum toxins for non-cosmetic uses.
  • Compare and contrast product characteristics, such as dosage, duration of effect, and preparation of available botulinum toxin products as they may impact formulary decisions.

Target Audience

This continuing pharmacy education activity was planned to meet the needs of decision makers in health-system pharmacy, including directors of pharmacy, associate and assistant directors of pharmacy, clinical coordinators, and supervisors, as well as clinical pharmacists. This activity would be especially beneficial for pharmacists involved in making formulary decisions and developing protocols related to botulinum toxin products or caring for patients who are candidates for or are already receiving botulinum toxin therapy.
